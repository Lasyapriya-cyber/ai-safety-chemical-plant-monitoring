# Dataset Analysis

## Tennessee Eastman Process Dataset

The Tennessee Eastman Process (TEP) dataset is one of the most widely used benchmark datasets for fault detection and process monitoring research in chemical engineering.

## Why This Dataset?

The dataset simulates a realistic chemical processing environment and contains multiple operating variables, control variables, and fault scenarios.

Key variables include:

- Reactor Temperature
- Reactor Pressure
- Feed Flow Rates
- Product Composition
- Cooling Water Flow
- Equipment Status

## Safety Relevance

Many industrial accidents are preceded by subtle deviations in process variables. Traditional threshold-based alarms may not capture these complex interactions.

Machine learning methods can potentially identify abnormal patterns before hazardous conditions become critical.

## Potential Failure Scenarios

- Reactor overheating
- Pressure build-up
- Cooling system malfunction
- Feed composition disturbances
- Equipment degradation

## Research Opportunity

By analyzing process data over time, AI systems may be able to provide earlier warning signals than conventional monitoring approaches, improving safety and reducing operational risk.
